    Our Story

    Where we began

    Esin Gumustekin was inspired by Dr. Ryuta Kawashima's study in Japan that suggested doing simple math and reading aloud for 30 mins a day, 5x a week, resulted in significant cognitive improvements for those with Alzheimer's.

    These activities stimulated the prefrontal cortex, where memory, cognition, and planning are controlled; participants who completed the exercises saw and felt improbemnts in communication, cognition, and mood.
